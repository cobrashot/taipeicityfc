Canada Day Beach Soccer Tournament

  • April 8, 2009

Here are some rough ideas for the Canada Day Beach Soccer Tournament.

Field

These are the dimensions for a single field.

Most soccer rules revolve around a 10-meter rule. In beach soccer it is 5 meters.

Goals

These are the dimensions of a beach soccer goal. Here is a picture of a goal on the beach in Rio de Janeiro. The above goal is bit smaller than what we we would need. With a larger goal we should use a modified design so the crossbar doesn’t hang down. Here is a more suitable design.  It would be portable enough to put in a van yet sturdy enough to support a 5-meter crossbar. I’ve already been to the neighborhood hardware store to price out 3" PVC piping and connectors.  As a rough estimate it would cost around 5,000-6,000 NT for a single goal. If there is enough room on the beach it would be a great tournament if we could have 2 fields/4 goals. With 2 fields we could invite 8 teams. 8 teams would mean around 70 players total.  And if you add up the additional friends and spectators the tournament would attract, it could bring a lot to the event. The refs can be arranged via the CTFA as they are quite accommodating and keen to promote soccer in Taiwan.  I will ask them if I can borrow corner flags from them on the day as well. Here’s the official Beach Soccer Rules from FIFA. [.pdf, 1.3 MB] I look forward to helping out however I can.  Let me know if you have any other ideas and hope to talk soon.
